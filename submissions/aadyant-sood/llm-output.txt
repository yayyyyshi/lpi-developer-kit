The SMILE methodology is a structured approach used to model real-world systems as digital twins. It breaks down systems into phases such as sensing, modeling, integration, learning, and execution.

In simple terms, SMILE helps in collecting data from real-world sources, processing it, and using it to create a virtual representation of that system. This digital twin can then be analyzed to understand patterns and generate insights.

For example, in a personal health system, data like sleep, energy, and stress can be collected and analyzed to create a digital twin of a person. This twin helps in identifying patterns and suggesting improvements.
