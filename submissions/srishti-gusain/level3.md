## Case Study 1: Digital Twin for AI Data Centers (NVIDIA + Schneider Industry use case)

Challenge: AI data centers require huge amounts of energy. Energy comparable to small cities. Power supply, computing, and storage lead to carbon emissions.

Approach: Organizations such as NVIDIA employ digital replicas of the entire data center. They simulate: power requirements, cooling systems, and handle AI loads.
Simply put, everything is modeled and tested even before any construction or operations take place.

Outcome: Optimal energy use. Lower carbon emissions. Enhanced cooling performance and infrastructure management. Adoption of green AI architecture.

Sources:
https://www.intelligentdatacentres.com/2025/02/17/building-a-greener-future-how-digital-twins-can-transform-data-centers-sustainability/


https://www.barrons.com/articles/nvidia-schneider-data-centers-stock-d21c3569


### SMILE Phase Mapping
Reality Emulation- A comprehensive digital copy of the data center is built, capturing servers, GPU, cooling process, and power consumption. Thus, engineers get an opportunity to simulate the effect of AI workload on power consumption without carrying out actual computation.

Concurrent Engineering- Different combinations of AI workload, cooling methods, and hardware use are tested virtually to find the configuration with minimum power consumption for further application.

Collective Intelligence- Information about the ongoing activities in the server room is collected by sensors and servers. This way, the current state of energy consumption and workload management is continuously visible.

Contextual Intelligence- The system makes adjustments based on the current situation. Workload distribution and cooling processes may be modified according to temperature and workload.

Continuous Intelligence- Future needs regarding power consumption are predicted. Possible actions include load balancing, hardware improvement, and schedule adjustment of training according to energy price.

Perpetual Wisdom- Organizations learn from each other, sharing experience and knowledge of the best practices related to energy-efficient design of AI data centers.


## Case Study 2: Energy Digital Twin in Smart Manufacturing (Heating Tunnel Case)
Challenge: Energy is wasted at an enormous scale in manufacturing processes, particularly during heating.

Approach: Designed a digital twin of the manufacturing system, which models the energy flow and heat distribution within machines. AI + control were applied to improve the process.

Outcome: Up to 40% decrease in energy use.

Source:
https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0736584524000152


### SMILE Phase Mapping
Reality Emulation- An emulated reality of the heating system is developed that takes into account temperature changes, machine operations, and energy consumption.

Concurrent Engineering- Various heating approaches and their operating parameters are analyzed to find out the most economical combination.

Collective Intelligence- Data about temperature, operation of machines, and energy consumption are collected through sensors at all times.

Contextual Intelligence- The heating intensity is controlled depending on the requirements of production processes and external factors.

Continuous Intelligence- Future energy requirements are predicted, and process optimization recommendations are generated to cut down on waste.

Perpetual Wisdom- The optimal strategies could be adopted for other production facilities.

## Case Study 3: AI + Digital Twin for Smart Buildings (Energy Optimisation)
Challenge: Too much energy is wasted in buildings owing to ineffective heating/cooling and lack of resource management.

Approach: Implemented AI-based digital twins of buildings through IoT sensors and machine learning models that forecasted and optimized energy consumption.

Outcome: 25–30% reduction in energy costs.

Source:
https://www.researchgate.net/publication/388370180_AI-Enhanced_Digital_Twins_for_Energy_Efficiency_and_Carbon_Footprint_Reduction_in_Smart_City_Infrastructure


### SMILE Phase Mapping
Reality Emulation - Creation of a digital replica of the building that includes HVAC systems, occupancy information, and energy consumption.


Concurrent Engineering - Multiple solutions for managing energy usage by using HVAC are simulated simultaneously.

Collective Intelligence - IoT devices gather real-time data about the temperature, occupancy levels, and energy usage.

Contextual Intelligence - Heating and cooling, as well as the lighting system, are managed according to the current situation.

Continuous Intelligence - Artificial Intelligence calculates future energy demands and optimizes resources to decrease its consumption.

Perpetual Wisdom - All of these energy-saving methods become part of smart cities' infrastructure.

## Conclusion
From the above case studies, it is clear that the digital twins facilitate how decisions are taken through enabling simulation testing within a virtual environment before implementing anything into the physical world. Instead of taking a trial and error approach that requires much time and resources, organizations get an opportunity to analyze multiple scenarios and select the most effective one in advance. Besides, the approach saves computational resources and minimizes environmental effects that emerge in the course of conducting big scale calculations. The combination of the two approaches makes the process much more methodical and systematic. Under SMILE, a specific procedure from setting the goal to enhancing system performance with the help of predictive analytics and live information is performed. In doing so, AI based solutions are developed in such a manner as to provide performance while being resource saving and environmentally friendly.
